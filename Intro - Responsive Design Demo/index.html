About Me

I have been fascinated with computers since I was old enough to use them. When I got my first video game console, after the novelty of having a new toy subsided my first thoughts revolved around how it worked. I even went as far as calling Sega's customer support hotline to ask. The person I spoke with was as helpful as they could have been, but even the trivial explanation they were able to give me sparked further intrigue in my young mind.

When I got my first Windows 98 machine in 1999 that intrigue finally took form. I along with a few friends began dabbling with an open source game development program called "RPG Toolkit". The interesting thing about this program was that it not only had support for user-made plugins written in C++ (which at the time I was blissfully unaware of) but utilized its own rudimentary programming language called "RPGCode" for organizing and scripting.

Though my friends and I never managed to pull together a complete game, the experience stuck with me and of all things inspired me to try my hand at learning web design. I built my first simple website at 14 utilizing basic HTML. From there I learned about BBS software such as phpBB and vBulletin and dabbled in the former, learning more about server-side programming and server maintenance in the process. I took what I had learned and began building websites for friends and family and on occasion would get paid for my efforts.

I graduated high school in 2004 a few years after the dotcom bubble burst. Not seeing a future in web development, I opted instead to pursue a degree in Human Service and Psychology--the latter being another subject I found fascinating. After a few years working towards that, familial obligations found me both sidelined and relocated to Texas, followed by Oregon. Upon moving to Oregon I realized that the tech industry had stabilized and thus took the opportunity to dive back into my true passion. I enrolled at Eastern Oregon University in 2013 pursuing a degree in Computer Science which I am on the cusp of acheiving.

In 2017, I encountered back-to-back family tragedies. Both of my parents were diagnosed with cancer. My mother was diagnosed with stage 4 liver cancer in June 2017 and 2 weeks after her passing in August 2017, my father was diagnosed with a grade 4 glioblastoma on his basal ganglia. He passed in February of 2018. My brother and I took care of both of them through these tribulations and as a result I ended up taking a year off from school. However in 2018 I resumed my studies and will be graduating in June 2019.
